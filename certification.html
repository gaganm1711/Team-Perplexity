Startup Certifications and Registrations in India

Essential Certifications for Your Startup Journey

1. Company Registration Certificate

Register your business with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) to obtain the Certificate of Incorporation. This legalizes your business structure.

Options include:

  • Private Limited Company
  • Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)
  • One Person Company (OPC)

2. PAN & TAN Registration

Permanent Account Number (PAN) and Tax Deduction and Collection Account Number (TAN) are necessary for tax filings and compliance.

Both PAN and TAN can be obtained from the Income Tax Department.

3. GST Registration

If your business turnover exceeds the prescribed threshold, GST registration is mandatory. Even if not compulsory, many startups register for GST to claim input tax credits.

Thresholds for GST Registration:

  • ₹40 lakh for goods
  • ₹20 lakh for services

Benefits of GST Registration:

  • Claim input tax credits
  • Enhanced business credibility
  • Ability to do interstate sales

4. Startup India Registration

Register on the Startup India portal for eligibility to access benefits such as tax exemptions, easier compliance, and funding opportunities.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • Innovative, scalable, and relatively young company

5. Udyam Registration (for MSMEs)

Register under the Udyam scheme for Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) to access benefits like easier loans and government schemes.

Benefits for MSMEs:

  • Access to government schemes
  • Easier access to credit and funding

6. Industry-Specific Licenses and Certifications

Some industries require additional certifications:

  • FSSAI License for food and beverage sector
  • Drug License for pharmaceutical or nutraceutical startups
  • BIS Certification for specific product standards
  • Environmental Clearances for certain industries

7. Other Certifications

Additional certifications may include:

  • ISO Certifications (e.g., ISO 9001, ISO 27001)
  • Cybersecurity Certifications for tech startups

8. Industry-Specific Requirements

Review the regulatory requirements specific to your industry, such as healthcare, technology, or manufacturing.

Additional requirements based on:

  • Healthcare
  • Tech Startups
  • Export/Import Businesses (IEC registration)
