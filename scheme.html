Government Schemes for Startups in India

Empowering Innovation & Entrepreneurship

1. Startup India Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS)

Provides financial support to startups for prototype development, market entry, and commercialization.

Details: The Startup India Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS) aims to provide financial assistance to startups for proof of concept, prototype development, product trials, market-entry, and commercialization [3]. This would enable these startups to graduate to a level where they will be able to raise investments from angel investors or venture capitalists or seek loans from commercial banks or financial institutions [3].

Seed Fund Disbursement:

  • Up to ₹20 Lakhs as a grant for validation of Proof of Concept, or prototype development, or product trials.
  • Up to ₹50 Lakhs of investment for market entry, commercialization, or scaling up through convertible debentures or debt or debt-linked instruments.

Eligibility: Early-stage startups meeting specific criteria. Visit Startup India Portal for detailed eligibility.

2. Credit Guarantee Scheme for Startups (CGSS)

Ensures collateral-free loans to startups, reducing financial risks and improving credit access.

Details: The Credit Guarantee Scheme for Startups (CGSS) provides credit guarantees to eligible startups, making it easier for them to secure financing without collateral.

Eligibility: Startups recognized/registered under DPIIT.

3. Venture Capital Assistance Scheme (VCA)

Provides financial support to startups for innovative agricultural projects.

Details: The Venture Capital Assistance Scheme (VCA) supports agri-startups with viable business models by providing financial assistance for innovative agricultural projects.

Eligibility: Agri-startups with a viable business model. Check NABARD for more details.

4. Atal Incubation Centre (AIC)

A startup funding scheme initiated by NITI Aayog to support entrepreneurs by covering their capital and operational expenditures [1].

Details: The Atal Incubation Centre (AIC) scheme, started in 2016, aims to support entrepreneurs by covering their capital and operational expenditures [1]. Approved startups can avail up to Rs. 10 crore over a five-year term [1].

Eligibility: Researchers, students, and startup owners across different areas of business, including public-private partnerships, public organizations, or completely private organizations [1]. A ready-to-use space of around 10,000 square feet is needed for the exclusive use of the AIC [1].

5. PSB59

A scheme that likely facilitates loans up to INR 59 lakh for startups, although specific details would need to be confirmed via official sources [1].

Details: While the specifics of PSB59 require verification, it generally aims to provide financial assistance to startups through easier loan access.

Eligibility: Startups meeting the criteria set by participating Public Sector Banks. Further details can be found on the official PSB59 scheme portal.

6. Production Linked Incentive (PLI) Scheme

A scheme to boost domestic manufacturing and attract large investments in key sectors [1].

Details: The Production Linked Incentive (PLI) Scheme aims to give incentives to companies for enhancing their manufacturing capabilities and increasing production in India [1].

Eligibility: Companies in specific sectors identified by the government. Refer to the official guidelines for each sector under the PLI scheme.
